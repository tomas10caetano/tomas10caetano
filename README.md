# About Me & Interests

Hi, I'm Tomás Caetano, a 3rd-year Engineering Physics student at the University of Porto and a Quantum Computing Developer.

My academic journey is defined by bridging the gap between theoretical many-body physics and practical quantum implementation. I actively take initiative in the field, having strengthened my foundation through the Qiskit Global Summer School, the ETH Zurich Summer School on Quantum Computing, and the JUNIQ/EPIQ Quantum Computing Summer School at the Jülich Supercomputing Center.

  Current Research & Projects
I am currently developing advanced simulations of open quantum systems, focusing on:

Quantum Many-Body Physics: Simulating non-equilibrium dynamics and Quantum Scars in Rydberg atom arrays (PXP Model) and Transverse Field Ising Models.

Error Mitigation: Benchmarking Zero-Noise Extrapolation (ZNE) against AI-driven correction (Random Forest regression) to mitigate decoherence in NISQ devices.

Hamiltonian Simulation: Implementing first-order Trotterization and comparing digital quantum simulations against exact diagonalization methods.

  Future Goals
